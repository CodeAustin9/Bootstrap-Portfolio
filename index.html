About Me

Originally from the midwest, I was born in Ann Arbor, Michigan. I have lived and taught in Colorado for the past eighteen years. My undergraduate degree was in Education and Humanities. After twenty years in education, I chose a new path - a path that lead me towards engineering and technology.

My new path began eight years ago. A concern of mine has always been the environment, and more specifically, climate change and human effects on the top of our land food chain, the polar bear. I traveled to Churchill, Canada, to get a first hand look at human impact on the polar bear habitat. What I saw was alarming, no ice and wintry landscape when these conditions should have been present. I spoke with biologists at the Polar Bear Research facility about an idea I had to help the polar bears, but my idea was met with speculation. This is when I decided that I would go back to school to become an engineer to try to build my prototype, but would need a lot of coursework and background to accomplish this goal in the meantime.

I'm still deciding what specific engineering degree I want to pursue to help build my prototype. I decided to enroll in the University of Denver Coding Bootcamp to figure out if I could somehow incorporate programming skills in with engineering. In addition to bootcamp, I am currently taking Calculus 3 and Intro to Python Programming at night. I plan to gain employment in the web development field upon graduation from bootcamp while attending Colorado School of Mines, either full time or part time, in August 2018.
